## **"The Pusher"** — Skit Treatment

### **COLD OPEN — The Wreckage**

Bedroom. Pre-dawn blue light. Total silence.

SLAM — alarm. Our guy, **DAVE**, 30s, bolts upright like he's been tased. Hair destroyed. One eye won't open. He slaps at the nightstand like he's trying to kill a wasp, knocking his phone off twice before silencing it.

He cradles his head with both hands. A low, wounded groan.

_Quick flashback montage — the night before:_ chaotic snippets. A round of shots arriving at a bar, everyone cheering. Dave attempting to explain something very serious using bar napkins. Someone falling off a barstool in slow motion. A blurry group selfie where half the faces are wrong. A cab ride where someone is definitely eating a hot dog they shouldn't have.

Back to Dave. Silence. He stares at the ceiling like a man accepting his fate.

His eyes drift to the nightstand. The remote. A single button glows faintly.

He reaches for it. Clicks it.

---

### **THE BED AWAKENS**

A soft mechanical hum. The back of the bed begins to rise. Slowly. Dignified, almost.

Dave rides it up, half-asleep, one arm dangling. It reaches sitting position and... keeps going. Dave's eyebrows raise slightly. It passes 90 degrees. He's now leaning forward. Then further. A look of dawning concern.

The hum doesn't stop.

He's standing. He's _past_ standing. The bed is now essentially a vertical wall behind him, and it is **still moving**, pressing gently but firmly into his back, walking him forward like a slow-moving forklift.

He looks behind him. Looks forward. Looks at the remote. Clicks the button again. Nothing changes.

The bed is in charge now.

---

### **THE GAUNTLET**

Each location gets its own little beat, the bed always humming steadily in the background:

**The Bathroom:** Dave gets pushed through the door sideways. Somehow he brushes his teeth during this. He's pushed directly into the shower — water turns on, he sputters, the bed waits just outside the shower curtain, still humming. The moment he turns the water off it nudges forward again.

**The Closet:** He's pushed through at moderate speed. Arms flail. He emerges in a shirt and one shoe. The bed reverses slightly, nudges him back in. He comes out with both shoes. The bed resumes.

**CUT TO:** Kitchen. The coffee maker timer kicks on. A perfect, glorious pot begins to brew. Peaceful. Quiet. The contrast is jarring.

**The Kitchen:** The bed is moving faster now. Dave sees the coffee. He reaches. It's _just_ out of reach. He strains. The bed does not care. He manages to grab the mug but the pour is chaotic — coffee splashing, him holding the mug at a 45-degree angle, desperately drinking what he can while being marched toward the front door.

He makes it out with maybe 60% of a cup of coffee. A small victory.

---

### **THE COMMUTE**

The bed pushes him out the front door and down the driveway. His **CAR** is there.

The bed lines him up perfectly with the driver's door. Pushes him in. He lands behind the wheel. The bed somehow wedges itself in behind him — half in the car, still humming.

**Driving scenes:**

- A red light. A city bus pulls up alongside him. Passengers stare. Dave gives a tired wave. One kid presses their face against the glass.
- A drive-through coffee attempt. The bed pushes him past the window before he can grab the cup. He watches it disappear in the rearview mirror.
- He tries to change the radio station. The bed bumps him, he accidentally hits scan, it cycles through stations endlessly.
- A cop clocks him, starts to pull him over, pulls up alongside, looks at the bed, slowly falls back and pretends he didn't see anything.

---

### **THE OFFICE**

Pushed through the parking lot. Pushed through the lobby. Coworkers part like the Red Sea.

He arrives at his desk and the bed nudges him into his chair and... idles. Still humming. Still there. But giving him just enough room to work, like a very committed personal assistant.

**Office scenes:**

- A meeting. Dave sits at the conference table. The bed is behind his chair. Nobody acknowledges it except one new employee who raises their hand to ask and gets silenced by a look from a coworker — _don't_.
- His boss stops by. Looks at the bed. Looks at Dave. "Big night?" Dave nods. The boss nods back. Walks away. He gets it.

**Then — his buddy MARCUS walks in.** Also looking rough. Dave looks up. They make eye contact.

Marcus also has a bed.

The two beds face each other for a moment. A beat of silence. Dave and Marcus share a long, exhausted, deeply understanding look. No words needed. The nod of two men who made the same choices.

---

### **THE WIND DOWN**

As the day goes on, the bed gradually slows. By 2pm it's barely a nudge. By 4pm it's just kind of resting against him. By 4:45 he's moving around the office on his own and the bed is just sort of parked nearby, engine idling quietly.

He almost looks... fond of it.

**5:00 PM.** Phone buzzes. It's the group chat.

**MARCUS:** _yo. Round 2?_

Three more names light up with thumbs up emojis in quick succession.

Dave stares at the phone. Looks at the bed. The bed's motor lets out one small, tired chirp — almost like a warning.

He types back: _"in"_

He looks at the bed. Pats it on the headboard like a loyal dog.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

_The hum resumes._

---

### **A Few Extra Gag Options**

- The bed has a **brand sticker** on the side that you catch briefly: something like _"RiseTech Pro — For People Who Need A Little Push"_
- At some point Dave tries to **sit on the bed** mid-commute and it immediately resumes moving
- End credits roll over the bed **making itself** back at the apartment, quietly, waiting
