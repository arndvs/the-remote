## PRODUCTION PHILOSOPHY

This video is made by a company that believes in what it has built. It does not acknowledge absurdity. It does not wink. It states facts in the order they become relevant and stops when the facts are done.

The humor — if the audience finds it — is entirely their own. RISE™ is not in on the joke. RISE™ is the joke's straight man, and the straight man never breaks.

**Tone references:** Apple's earliest iPhone teasers. Dyson product films. The Rivian launch video. Companies that know something you don't yet, and are comfortable with that. And now — with the track — something with more swagger. Still earnest. Just moving.

## ACTUATOR CLOSE-UPS — THE BEAUTY SHOTS

These are the video's visual backbone. Shot like a luxury car commercial or a Swiss watch film — macro lenses, controlled lighting, slow motion. The mechanism doing exactly what it was engineered to do, in loving detail.

**What we're shooting:**

**Shot A — The Stair Actuator Engaging:**
Extreme close-up. The Move's stair navigation mechanism making first contact with a step edge. The precision of the initial engagement — the actuator finding the geometry of the step, locking in. Warm directional light from one side. The metal surface catching it. Shot at 240fps, played back at 24fps — ten times slower than reality. Every micron of movement visible.

**Shot B — Mid-Stair Transit, Side Profile:**
Close-up from the side. The actuator mechanism cycling through a step — the controlled transfer of load, the geometry resolving, the next contact point finding its position. The material quality of the mechanism in motion. This is the shot that makes engineers feel things.

**Shot C — The Step Edge:**
Extreme close-up on a single stair's edge — the nosing of the step — as the Move's base crosses it. The transition from tread to rise. The actuator reading the geometry in real time. The edge of the step passing under the mechanism the way a ship's hull passes under water. Slow. Inevitable.

**Shot D — The Rail Detail:**
Close-up on the Move's frame rail during stair transit. The surface texture. The RISE™ branding element on the rail — small, precise. The rail under load, resolving. The material confidence of something built to do this.

**Shot E — The Top Landing Arrival:**
The Move completing a flight — the moment the last step is cleared and flat navigation resumes. The mechanism transitioning from stair mode to caster mode. Shot from slightly above, slow motion, the base of the Move crossing the landing threshold.

**Shot F — Solo Descent, Dawn:**
The most cinematic actuator shot. The Move on the stairs alone, before dawn. Low light — a single landing fixture, warm against the dark stairwell. Close on the actuator mechanism descending a step — controlled, deliberate, alone. Nobody watching. Nobody needed.

**Lighting for all actuator shots:**
Single source, warm directional. Creates deep shadows in the mechanical detail. No flat lighting. No studio white. These shots should feel like they were lit by someone who loves mechanisms.

## EDIT NOTES

**Cut to the beat, always.** Every shot change lands on a downbeat or a snare hit. The actuator shots sync to the track's rhythm — the mechanism moving with the music, not alongside it.

**The "Until the stairs" shot holds through two full bars.** The track runs. The bed is still. The stillness against the moving track is the video's central tension. Do not cut early.

**The actuator shots are the verses. The wide product shots are the choruses.** Alternate between them. The macro detail builds credibility. The wide shots land the emotion.

**"We appreciate your original purchase" lands mid-track.** Noted, absorbed, the track moving on immediately. The weight of it comes from how quickly the edit moves past it.

**Dr. Voss gets the silence.** The track fades for her. She doesn't compete with Ludacris. She doesn't need to.

## THE LUDACRIS CONNECTION

*The Remote* features Dave whistling **"Move Bitch"** by Ludacris twice. The song finds him on the car radio — just the intro whistle, no lyrics. He picks it up unconsciously, whistling along as he pulls out of the driveway with the bed folded in his back seat. Then the fuel gauge registers — past empty, warning light decorative at this point — and the whistle stops mid-phrase. He kills the radio. Says "yeah" to nobody. Keeps driving. Two coffee denials and one dead car later, the gas station beat pays off what the whistle planted.

At 5PM, heading for the elevator, Dave whistles it again. The same phrase. Complete this time. Unhurried. The morning interrupted it. The afternoon earned its resolution.

*The Move* teaser runs **"Stand Up"** by Ludacris ft. Shawnna — *"when I move you move, just like that"* — the product's entire thesis in one lyric.

Both Ludacris. Both movement. Both completely earnest in context. The connection is never stated. It doesn't need to be. The audience who knows both pieces feels it. RISE™ didn't plan this. Dave didn't plan this. The universe just runs on Ludacris.
